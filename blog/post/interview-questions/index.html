Some not-technical interview questions

27 Aug 2018, 04:36

I’ve mostly been interviewed and have assisted during a few interviews but I haven’t had a chance to interview someone yet but when I do, these are some of my favorite questions to ask during the second half of an exhaustive technical interview.

  • Which websites and blogs do you follow to keep up with tech news?

    • Websites, blogs, YouTube channels, books and news streams are how we learn every day. This question makes the candidate talk about their daily sources of tech news and information.

  • Explain something trivial that you learned last week.

    • Talking about something trivial yet interesting that they encountered last week, shows how they’re inclined towards problem solving and active learning all the time. This also gives an insight into how well they can retain and explain trivial stuff and transfer knowledge to others.

  • What’s your favorite thing to do when you get stuck while solving a problem?

    • The answer could be anything ranging from “go out for a walk” to “grab coffee” or “seek help from peers”. The point of this question is to understand how the candidate handles being stuck.

  • When was the last time you had a disagreement over tech, at work and how did you handle it?

    • Innovation happens when different people come together and when different people come together, disagreements are bound to happen too. This question puts the candidate in a tough spot but helps the interviewer gain insight into how well the candidate can handle disagreements and come out of it with a solution.

  • Tell me something about yourself.

    • This one’s a classic and is always a great question to ask because it makes candidates talk about themselves as a person.
